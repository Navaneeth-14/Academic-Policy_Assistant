[ATTENDANCE]
Students must maintain a minimum of 75% attendance per semester. Attendance is calculated based on the total number of classes held versus classes attended.

[CONDONATION]
Attendance between 65% and 74% may be condoned with a valid medical certificate submitted within 7 days of returning to class. Condonation is granted only once per academic year.

[EXAM ELIGIBILITY]
Students below 65% attendance are not eligible to appear for semester examinations without prior written approval from the principal. Appeals must be submitted at least 10 days before the exam.

[GRADING POLICY]
The grading system uses a 10-point scale. A grade of 8.0 and above is considered distinction. Students scoring below 4.0 in any subject must repeat the course in the next available semester.

[INTERNAL ASSESSMENT]
Internal assessment contributes 40% to the final grade. It includes two mid-term tests, assignments, and attendance. Missing an internal assessment without prior approval results in a zero for that component.

[MAKEUP EXAM]
Students who miss a semester examination due to a medical emergency may apply for a makeup exam within 5 days of the original exam date. A medical certificate from a registered practitioner is mandatory.

[ACADEMIC PROBATION]
Students who fail more than two subjects in a semester are placed on academic probation. Probation students must meet with their academic advisor within the first two weeks of the following semester.

[PLAGIARISM]
Submitting work that is not your own, including copying from peers or the internet without citation, is considered plagiarism. First offense results in a zero for the assignment. Second offense leads to disciplinary action.

[COURSE WITHDRAWAL]
Students may withdraw from a course without academic penalty before the end of the fourth week of the semester. Withdrawals after this period are recorded as a failing grade unless approved by the academic committee.

[SCHOLARSHIP ELIGIBILITY]
Students must maintain a GPA of 8.5 or above and have no disciplinary record to be eligible for merit scholarships. Scholarship applications open in the third week of each semester.
